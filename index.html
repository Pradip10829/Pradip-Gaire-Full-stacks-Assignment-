1) What Is Full Stack Development?

Full stack development is the practice of building complete web applications across three connected layers: front-end (user interface), back-end (business logic and APIs), and database (persistent data). A full stack developer understands how data flows between these layers and can design solutions that are usable, secure, and maintainable.

In professional projects, this includes selecting the right architecture (monolith or microservices), defining API contracts, validating data, handling authentication/authorization, and deploying software through CI/CD.

Core Competencies Demonstrated

  • End-to-end request/response lifecycle understanding.
  • Separation of concerns between UI, server, and data.
  • Awareness of non-functional requirements: security, performance, scalability.
